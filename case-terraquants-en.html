Context

TerraQuants is a platform initiative led by an experienced investor. The goal: bring together data from multiple sources to support better decision-making and make it accessible through a platform + community, together with external specialists (dev/data/design).

The team consisted of web developers, data analysts, and designers working in a hybrid setup on a project basis, spread across multiple countries.

Challenge

The team consisted of web developers, data analysts and designers die hybride en op projectbasis werkten. The client had limited time alongside his regular work, maar wilde wel grip op voortgang, dependencies and decision-making.

Because the team was distributed across meerdere landen (Pakistan, India, Griekenland en Nederland), alignment, handovers, and decisions needed to be tightly organized.

Without clear structure, questions and updates threatened to cause noise, waardoor snelheid en focus onder druk kwamen te staan.

Assignment

Set up a collaboration and communication flow with which the team could continue independently, while progress and decisions remained clear voor de opdrachtgever.

Approach / What I set up

Slack

Communication & alignment

Notion

Documentation & progress tracking

1. Slack structure

  • Project/discipline channels for collaboration
  • General channels for updates and alignment
  • Fixed moments for team calls and short check-ins

2. Notion as the central hub

  • All documentation in one place (agreements, decisions, plans)
  • Overview of tasks/progress per discipline
  • Transparency in status and next steps

3. Workflows for questions and alignment

  • Clear route: where to ask which question, and to whom
  • Reduced noise and duplicate work

4. Decision pyramid

Decision levels:

  • Team: What can the team decide themselves? (operational choices, technical implementation)
  • Project Leader: When does it escalate to me? (scope, priorities, conflicts)
  • Client: When must the client decide? (strategy, budget, direction)

This enabled faster execution without unnecessary escalation.

International collaboration

With team members in Pakistan, India, Greece and the Netherlands it was essential to:

  • Set up asynchronous communication well (not everyone is online at the same time)
  • Have clear documentation where everyone can work independently
  • Plan fixed check-in moments that work for all time zones
  • Bridge cultural differences in work style with explicit agreements

Result / Delivery

  • The team could work in parallel with clear alignment and fewer misunderstandings
  • Progress became visible and consistent through Notion + structured Slack updates
  • The client retained control with minimal time investment
  • Faster decision-making thanks to clear mandates (decision pyramid)
  • Less noise and duplicate work through structured communication
